         <ul>
           <li>September 9, 1941: Otis Ray Redding Jr. was born in Dawson, Georgia, U.S. 
</li>
           <li>1958: Redding joined Johnny Jenkins's band, the Pinetoppers, touring the Southern states as a singer and driver. 
</li>
           <li>1962: An unscheduled appearance at a Stax Records recording session led to a contract and his first hit single, "These Arms of Mine". 
</li>
           <li>1964: Stax released Redding's debut album, Pain in My Heart. 
</li>
           <li>1965: He released Otis Blue/Otis Redding Sings Soul, which became a crossover success and one of his best-selling LPs. 
</li>
           <li>1966: Redding released Complete & Unbelievable: The Otis Redding Dictionary of Soul, featuring hits like "Try a Little Tenderness". 
</li>
           <li>June 1967: Performed at the Monterey Pop Festival, gaining widespread acclaim and introducing his music to a broader audience. 
</li>
           <li>December 10, 1967: Tragically died in a plane crash in Madison, Wisconsin, at the age of 26. 
</li>
           <li>January 1968: "(Sittin' On) The Dock of the Bay" was released posthumously, becoming the first posthumous number-one record on both the Billboard Hot 100 and R&B charts.</li>
           <li>Posthumous Recognition: Redding received numerous accolades after his death, including two Grammy Awards, the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award, and induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.</li>
         </ul>
